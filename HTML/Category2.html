Financial Support Schemes

PM-Kisan Samman Nidhi Yojana

The Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi Yojana (PM-Kisan) is a flagship government scheme launched by the Government of India to provide direct income support to small and marginal farmers across the country. Under the scheme, eligible farmers receive financial assistance of Rs. 6,000 per year, provided in three equal installments of Rs. 2,000 each. The aim of PM-Kisan is to ensure a minimum fixed income to farmers, thereby supporting their livelihoods and helping them meet their agricultural expenses.
To be eligible for PM-Kisan, farmers must have cultivable landholding of up to 2 hectares. The scheme excludes higher-income farmers, institutional landholders, and certain other categories of individuals. Farmers can register for the scheme through designated nodal officers or online via the PM-Kisan portal, providing basic details such as Aadhaar number, bank account information, and landholding particulars.

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maandhan Yojana(PMKMY)

The Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maandhan Yojana (PM-KMY) is a government-backed pension scheme launched by the Government of India specifically for small and marginal farmers in the country. The scheme aims to provide social security and financial support to farmers in their old age by offering them a pension after attaining the age of 60 years.
Under PM-KMY, eligible farmers between the ages of 18 and 40 years can voluntarily enroll in the scheme. Upon enrollment, farmers need to contribute a specified amount towards the pension fund at regular intervals. The central government also contributes an equal amount to the pension fund on behalf of the farmer. After attaining the age of 60 years, enrolled farmers become eligible to receive a monthly pension.
The monthly pension amount provided under PM-KMY is fixed at Rs. 3,000. Additionally, enrolled farmers also receive a life insurance cover of Rs. 2 lakhs in case of death during the period of coverage. The scheme offers portability, allowing enrolled farmers to continue receiving benefits even if they migrate to other locations within the country.

Jan Dhan Yojana

The Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY), commonly known as the Jan Dhan Yojana, is a financial inclusion scheme launched by the Government of India in August 2014. The primary objective of the scheme is to provide access to financial services such as banking and insurance to the unbanked population in India.
PMJDY has been instrumental in expanding financial inclusion in India, with millions of previously unbanked individuals gaining access to formal banking services. The scheme has contributed to reducing financial untouchability, promoting savings habits, and facilitating government welfare programs more efficiently.

National Agriculture Market (e-NAM)

The National Agriculture Market (e-NAM) is an ambitious initiative launched by the Government of India in April 2016 with the aim of creating a unified national market for agricultural commodities. The e-NAM platform is an online trading portal that facilitates transparent and efficient trading of agricultural produce across various mandis (market yards) in the country.
The National Agriculture Market (e-NAM) aims to revolutionize agricultural marketing in India by leveraging technology to create a transparent, efficient, and competitive marketplace for agricultural commodities, thereby benefiting farmers, traders, and consumers alike.
