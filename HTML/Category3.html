Crop Field Schemes

Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)

The Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) is a government scheme implemented by the Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, Government of India. Launched in 2015, PKVY aims to promote organic farming practices among farmers and encourage the adoption of traditional and indigenous agricultural techniques. Financial aid of Rs. 50,000 per hectare/ three years is allowed, out of which Rs. 31,000 (61 percent) is given to the farmer directly.

Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY)

The Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) is a flagship scheme launched by the Government of India on 1st July 2015, aimed at providing comprehensive irrigation solutions to farmers across the country. With a focus on enhancing water use efficiency and expanding irrigation coverage, PMKSY seeks to address the challenges of water scarcity in agriculture.
PMKSY comprises various components such as Accelerated Irrigation Benefit Program (AIBP), Har Khet Ko Pani (Water to Every Field), Per Drop More Crop, Integrated Watershed Management Program, Renovation of Water Bodies, and Command Area Development. These components aim to ensure access to irrigation water through efficient techniques like micro-irrigation and drip irrigation, while also promoting sustainable agricultural practices.

Micro Irrigation Fund (MIF)

The Micro Irrigation Fund (MIF) is a financial assistance program initiated by the Government of India to promote the adoption of micro-irrigation techniques among farmers. The primary objective of the Micro Irrigation Fund is to encourage farmers to shift from traditional flood irrigation methods to more efficient and water-saving micro-irrigation systems such as drip irrigation and sprinkler irrigation.Provide states with concessional rate of interest on required amount in order to promote micro-irrigation.

New Jal Shakti Ministry

The Ministry of Jal Shakti is responsible for the overall planning, coordination, and management of water resources in India. Its primary objectives include ensuring the sustainable management of water resources, promoting water conservation and efficiency, facilitating integrated water resources management, and addressing challenges related to water scarcity, pollution, and access to safe drinking water. To provide piped water connection to every Indian household by the year 2024.

Soil Health Card Scheme

The Soil Health Card Scheme (SHC) is a government initiative launched by the Government of India to assess and improve soil health and fertility across the country. It was launched in February 2015 as part of the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) under the Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare. Under this, the govt plans to issue soil cards to farmers which will carry crop-wise recommendations of nutrients and fertilisers required for the individual farms.

Neem Coated Urea Scheme

The Neem Coated Urea Scheme is an initiative introduced by the Government of India to improve the efficiency of urea fertilizer usage in agriculture. Under this scheme, urea fertilizer is coated with neem (Azadirachta indica) extracts before distribution to farmers. The scheme was launched in 2015 to address several issues related to urea usage in agriculture. To boost the growth of wheat and paddy, and reduce the black marketeering and hoarding or urea.

National Programme on Organic Production (NPOP)

The National Programme on Organic Production (NPOP) is a scheme launched by the Government of India to promote organic farming practices and ensure the integrity of organic products in the country. NPOP was introduced in 2000 under the Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, with the primary objective of facilitating the development of organic agriculture and setting standards for organic production and certification. `Development of organic value chains in the North East Region of India and promote organic farming.

Mission Organic Chain Development for the North Eastern Region (MOVCDNER)

The Mission Organic Chain Development for the North Eastern Region (MOVCDNER) is an initiative launched by the Government of India to promote organic farming in the northeastern states of the country. The program aims to harness the agricultural potential of the region while emphasizing sustainable and eco-friendly agricultural practices.
