Insurance Schemes

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

The Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) is a crop insurance scheme launched by the Government of India. It aims to provide financial support to farmers in the event of crop failure due to natural calamities, pests, or diseases. The scheme replaces the earlier crop insurance schemes such as the National Agricultural Insurance Scheme (NAIS) and the Modified National Agricultural Insurance Scheme (MNAIS).

Prime Minister Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana

The Prime Minister Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana (PMJJBY) is a government-backed life insurance scheme launched by the Government of India. It aims to provide affordable life insurance coverage to individuals in the age group of 18 to 50 years, thereby ensuring financial security for their families in case of untimely death. Life Insurance scheme with risk coverage of Rs. 2 Lakh in case of death of the insured, due to any reason.

Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana

The Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana (PMSBY) is an accidental death and disability insurance scheme launched by the Government of India. It aims to provide affordable insurance coverage to individuals against accidental death and disability, thereby ensuring financial security for them and their families in case of unfortunate events. An accident Insurance scheme, PMSBY offers a one-year accidental death and disability cover, which can be renewed annually.

Livestock Insurance Scheme

The Livestock Insurance Scheme is a government-backed initiative aimed at providing insurance coverage to livestock keepers in India. Livestock insurance helps mitigate the financial losses that farmers may incur due to the death of their animals resulting from natural calamities, diseases, or accidents. Provides protection mechanism to the farmers against any eventual loss of their animals due to death. Some schemes may also cover veterinary expenses incurred for treating insured animals in case of illness or injury, subject to certain terms and conditions

Weather Based Crop Insurance Scheme

The Weather-Based Crop Insurance Scheme (WBCIS) is an insurance scheme designed to provide financial support to farmers in the event of crop loss or damage caused by adverse weather conditions. Unlike traditional crop insurance schemes, which rely on crop cutting experiments and yield assessment, WBCIS bases its payouts on weather parameters such as rainfall, temperature, humidity, and other weather-related factors.

PM Kisan Mandhan Yojana

The Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maan-Dhan Yojana (PM-KMY) is a government-backed pension scheme launched by the Government of India specifically for small and marginal farmers in the country. The scheme aims to provide social security and financial support to farmers in their old age by offering them a pension after attaining the age of 60 years. The scheme relies on Aadhaar-linked identification to ensure accurate targeting and disbursement of benefits to eligible farmers.

National Agriculture Insurance Scheme

The National Agriculture Insurance Scheme (NAIS) was a crop insurance scheme launched by the Government of India to provide financial support to farmers in the event of crop failure due to natural calamities, pests, or diseases. However, it has been replaced by the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY), which offers more comprehensive coverage and features.
However, NAIS faced criticisms regarding its implementation, including issues related to delays in claim settlements, lack of transparency, and inadequate coverage. As a result, the government introduced the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) in 2016, which aimed to address the shortcomings of NAIS and provide more comprehensive and efficient crop insurance coverage to farmers across the country. PMFBY integrates the strengths of NAIS and other previous crop insurance schemes while introducing several improvements to enhance coverage, transparency, and efficiency in claim settlement.

Coconut Palm Insurance Scheme

The “Coconut Palm Insurance Scheme (CPIS)” is being implemented by the Coconut Development Board, Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, Government of India with the objective of insuring coconut palms against natural calamities, climatic risks, pests, diseases and other perils. Under this scheme, all healthy nut bearing coconut palms in the age group from 4 years to 60 years in a contiguous area (Mono/mixed) can be insured against natural perils leading to death/loss of palm/becoming unproductive. The scheme is being implemented in all coconut growing States through Agriculture Insurance Company and implementing State Governments.
